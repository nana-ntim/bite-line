Our Story

How we're bringing the soul back to food ordering, one meal at a time.

So What's The Problem?

Food is humanity's oldest connector, yet modern ordering strips away everything that makes it magical - the discovery, the relationships, the shared experience. We've turned breaking bread into breaking screens.

That's Where Bite-Line Comes In

Bite-Line brings the magic back, rebuilding food ordering around real connections between the people who cook and the communities they feed, where every order sparks conversation and turns strangers into neighbors.

We started on campus, testing if students and vendors wanted something more than just transactions. The answer was overwhelming - 183% order growth in our first campaigns, 51 people joining as beta testers, vendors asking to partner with us.

The Journey

Week 3-4

Research & Discovery

Interviewed 5 vendors and 10 students. Discovered that everyone was hungry for more than just convenience - they wanted connection.

Week 5-8

Building & Testing

Built the MVP. Onboarded our first vendors. Watched real people use the platform to order real food - and it worked.

Week 9-12

Validation

Ran 4 Fika campaigns. Orders grew 183%. 51 beta testers joined. Vendors asked to partner. The model works.

Now

Scaling

Campus proves it works. Ghana proves it scales. Then we go global - because people everywhere deserve food that connects, not just feeds.

What We Learned Along The Way

Marketing = Quality

Our desk study said it. Fika campaigns proved it. Even the best product needs people to know about it.

Feedback > Assumptions

Real behavior reveals what surveys hide. Watch users, don't just ask them.

Iterate Fast

Build-Measure-Learn cycles let you adapt faster than perfect planning ever could.

Community Wins

Social features create stickiness. Community keeps people coming back more than convenience alone.

What's Next

We start on campus, but we're building for everywhere - because the hunger for connection, for knowing who feeds you, for breaking bread together? That's not campus. That's human.
